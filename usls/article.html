USLS Breaks Ground for Tech Center at Granada Campus

A new era for Lasallian innovation begins with the Ramon F. Villavicencio AFSC Center for Technology.

Photo by Center for Marketing

The Briefing

  • Facility Name Ramon F. Villavicencio AFSC Center
  • Location Granada Campus (55 Hectares)
  • Scale 3-Story Technology Hub
  • Primary Focus Tech, Innovation & Research

Share Story

The University of St. La Salle marked a significant milestone in campus development with the groundbreaking of the Ramon F. Villavicencio AFSC Center for Technology on December 16 at its Granada Campus.

The soon-to-rise three-story facility will be built within the University's massive 55-hectare property. It is purposefully designed to serve students and faculty engaged in technology-related programs, marking a shift towards a more integrated, research-driven curriculum.

"A hub for creative learning, research, and collaboration, empowering students to explore science, technology, and innovation."

A Gathering of Visionaries

The ceremony was attended by members of the Villavicencio family and University administrators led by President Br. Joaquin Severino S. Martinez FSC, alongside the De La Salle Brothers Community – Bacolod.

Also present were members of the Board of Trustees, dedicated benefactors, and representatives from the College of Computing Studies, all gathering to witness this pivotal moment in the university's expansion.

Honoring Legacy

The structure reflects the enduring support of Br. Ramon “Chito” F. Villavicencio, AFSC, whose long-standing dedication to advancing access to quality Lasallian education has shaped numerous initiatives across La Salle institutions.

Once completed, the Center for Innovation and Technology will serve not just as a classroom building, but as a dynamic ecosystem for future-ready skills, bridging the gap between academic theory and industrial application within a faith-filled Lasallian environment.
